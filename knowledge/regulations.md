# Indian Insurance Regulations Reference

## Regulatory Authority

**IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India)**
- Established under IRDAI Act, 1999
- Regulates and supervises insurance industry in India
- Protects policyholder interests
- Website: www.irdai.gov.in

---

## Key Legislation

### Insurance Act, 1938
- Primary legislation governing insurance in India
- Section 45: Policy cannot be called into question after 3 years
- Section 64VB: No insurance without premium payment
- Amended multiple times, latest being 2021

### IRDAI (Health Insurance) Regulations, 2016
- Comprehensive regulations for health insurance
- Standardized policy terms and conditions
- Mandates claim settlement timelines
- Portability provisions

### Consumer Protection Act, 2019
- Policyholder can file complaint at Consumer Forum
- District Forum: Up to ₹1 crore claims
- State Commission: ₹1 crore to ₹10 crore
- National Commission: Above ₹10 crore

### Insurance Ombudsman Rules, 2017
- Alternative dispute resolution mechanism
- Free of cost to policyholders
- Claims up to ₹50 lakhs
- 17 Ombudsman centers across India

## Claim Settlement Timelines (IRDAI Mandated)

### Cashless Claims
- **Pre-authorization response**: Within 1 hour for planned, 2 hours for emergency
- **Initial response for discharge**: Within 2 hours
- **Final settlement**: Within 3 hours of final bill submission

### Reimbursement Claims
- **Acknowledgment**: Within 3 working days
- **Query (if any)**: Within 15 days of claim receipt
- **Settlement**: Within 30 days of receiving all documents
- **Interest penalty**: 2% above bank rate if delayed beyond 30 days

---

## Policyholder Rights Under IRDAI Guidelines

### Right to Information
1. Clear disclosure of policy terms before purchase
2. Policy document within 30 days
3. Written reasons for claim rejection
4. Access to all claim-related documents

### Right to Claim
1. Cashless facility at network hospitals
2. Reimbursement within stipulated timelines
3. Pre-existing disease coverage after waiting period
4. No claim rejection for non-disclosure after 8 years (moratorium period)

### Right to Appeal/Grievance
1. Internal grievance redressal within 15 days
2. IGMS portal complaint
3. Insurance Ombudsman
4. Consumer Forum
5. Civil Court

---

## Grievance Redressal Mechanism

### Level 1: Insurance Company GRO
- File complaint with Grievance Redressal Officer
- Response within 15 days
- Escalate to senior management if unresolved

### Level 2: IGMS Portal
- Integrated Grievance Management System
- Online complaint at igms.irda.gov.in
- IRDAI monitors resolution
- Response within 15 days

### Level 3: Insurance Ombudsman
- Free of cost
- No lawyer required
- Complaints up to ₹50 lakhs
- Decision within 3 months
- Binding on insurer, not on complainant

### Level 4: Consumer Forum
- File under Consumer Protection Act
- Can claim compensation for deficiency in service
- Mental agony compensation possible
- Interest on delayed settlement

## Common Valid Rejection Reasons

### Pre-existing Disease (PED)
- Diseases existing before policy inception
- 48 months waiting period typically
- Permanent exclusions may apply

### Waiting Period
- Initial waiting period: 30 days (except accidents)
- Specific disease waiting: 24-48 months
- Maternity waiting: 36-48 months

### Policy Exclusions
- Cosmetic/plastic surgery
- Dental treatment (unless due to accident)
- Infertility treatment
- Self-inflicted injuries
- War and nuclear risks

### Documentation Issues
- Incomplete documents
- Late intimation
- Non-disclosure of material facts

---

## Invalid/Challengeable Rejections

### IRDAI Circular Violations
1. Rejecting for PED after moratorium period (8 years)
2. Not giving written reasons for rejection
3. Delayed settlement without interest
4. Arbitrary rejection without investigation

### Policy Misinterpretation
1. Applying exclusions incorrectly
2. Ignoring waiting period completion
3. Wrong calculation of admissible amount
4. Denying covered procedures

### Procedural Violations
1. Not following claim investigation guidelines
2. No intimation to policyholder before rejection
3. Using TPA opinion without insurer review
4. Not considering medical opinion

---

## Key IRDAI Circulars for Appeals

### IRDAI/HLT/REG/CIR/174/08/2020
- COVID-19 claim settlement guidelines
- No blanket rejections

### IRDAI/HLT/MISC/CIR/073/03/2020
- Standard treatment guidelines
- Cannot deny based on treatment protocol

### IRDAI/HLT/REG/CIR/256/11/2020
- Moratorium Period: 8 years
- After 8 years, no rejection for non-disclosure

---

## Supporting Documents for Appeal

Strong appeals typically include:
1. Original claim rejection letter
2. Policy copy with relevant sections highlighted
3. All medical records and bills
4. Discharge summary
5. Doctor's certificate/letter
6. Previous claim settlement records (if any)
7. IRDAI circular references
8. Policy wordings showing coverage
9. Timeline documentation
10. Communication records with insurer/TPA
